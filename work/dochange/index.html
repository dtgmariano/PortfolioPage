What is it about?

The stakeholders of the projects requested my team to research a particular group of seniors in Eindhoven. These seniors participate in a weekly cooking activity at the Summa College. Further more, the stakeholders asked my team to develop a digital prototype for a smart table that would aid seniors to cook and socialize. What did we achieve by the end of the project?

As a result, we came across with many insightful factors such as:

1. the interactive table demands a lot of the user's visual process: the table's dimensions and the parallax perspective of items displayed diagonally on the table requires a lot of their peripheral vision;
2. the screen projection should be dynamically adjusted to the presence of artifacts over the counter (ingredients, cutlery, boards, etc.);
3. the cooking-aid feature must be flexible: users had different algorithms to execute the same task, and they were annoyed with the level of control during the trial;
4. advanced cooks, requires only basic aid: ingredients list, measurements, recipes;
5. users reported that seeing the photos of their friend's dishes would be an engaging factor in their food choices.

The outcomes of the project are to be used in the next projects of Reach 2020 Consortium. As deliverables, we provided prototype files, design guidelines and a presentation about the design case's findings.
