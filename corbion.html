BRIEFING

ABOUT THE CLIENT

Corbion is the global market leader in lactic acid, lactic acid derivatives, and a leading company in emulsifiers, functional enzyme blends, minerals, vitamins and algae ingredients. Their factory in Gorinchem runs various process line simultaneously 24/7. A central control room displays processes status and variables from sensors readings. As part of the "Control Room of the Future" Project, Corbion is addressing new ideas to improve the efficiency and safety of the processes from their operators perspective.

Panel and field operators can be assigned to one or more processes. Each operation has particular characteristics such as operation mode, localization, machinery, sensors, etc. Panel operator (PO) monitors the readings from sensors and observe processes status, while field operator (FO) performs check rounds, pick samples of batches, manual adjustments on machinery and inspect checkpoints for problems. POs are based at the central control room whereas FOs walk around the factory.

The interaction between operators is a crucial activity. They use radio communication to share: process status, parameters value, leakage notification, incoming tasks, and others. In conditions where communication fails, operators experience attention bottlenecks that could affect reactions to calls or delay a process. Therefore improving support to operators is critical to enhance the conditions of their activities.

UNDERSTANDING OPERATORS NEEDS

CONTEXTUAL TASK ANALYSIS, INTERVIEWS, GROUP SESSIONS, ONLINE SURVEY

During the project, my team performed weekly visits to the factory. We observed operators executing tasks during their work shift. Also, we conducted interviews, an online survey and a group session to discuss their priorities, tasks difficulties, and bottlenecks that could be improved. These activities allowed the prioritization of the design opportunities within the project scope.

COMMUNICATION & TASKLIST

ENHANCING COMMUNICATION AND PLANNING FOR OPERATORS

As one of the problems identified, the communication breakdown between operators happens on a daily base. Noise interference, no-signal areas or misplacement of the walkie-talkie were some of the reasons that lead to this issue. Besides that, field operators reported some problems with modifications in their plans during a shift. They usually plan their routes - across the entire facility - according to the tasks and checkpoints assigned to them. New incoming tasks cause route disruptions, increasing a task duration due to an additional walkthrough. Another situation where extra time occurs happens when field operators have to wait for panel operator updates - process status, sensor readings, time to pick a sample - to proceed with the remaining tasks.

We designed an application for a smartphone to support their communication and planning more efficiently. It provides an overview of activities, processes status, notifications and network communication.
