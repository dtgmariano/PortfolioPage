Daniel Mariano

User Experience Designer

What do I do?

I'm a User Experience Designer based in Eindhoven, The Netherlands, currently working within the User System Interaction program at TU/e. As an addition to my UX skills, I have a Biomedical and Electrical Engineering background.

My journey to UX

Hacking, Prototyping, Testing: From an engineering school to the UX world

Since college, I've always enjoyed expressing my ideas with digital prototypes, using electronics, microcontrollers, wires, and creativity. I've explored as much as I could: built real-time system applications, hack an old robot-arm firmware, controlled a pc's keyboard with an eye-tracker device, use physiological signal as input for UI control, and more!

I always believed that technology has an immense potential to change society. Nevertheless, design needs to take human behavior into account! I discovered this valuable lesson when I shift to a gig where the primary goal was to help ALS patients to communicate with their beloved ones.

I've started as a developer for Microsoft-based apps focused on Augmentative and Alternative Communication. Over time, I began to spend less time hacking at the lab and more time talking with caregivers, speech-language pathologists, doctors, or anyone concerned with the matter. I've started to hang at hospitals, home cares, rehabilitation facilities, or any place that I could observe and speak (with the help of communication boards) to a real user.

In other words, I left the Matrix to have a glimpse of the real world, and this change gave me a new perspective on life.

After several attempts (with successes and failures), it eventually hit me: a good product is good as its user's involvement during all design stages: research, ideation, prototype, test (*check this four items -> look UX mastery book).

In 2016 I moved to Eindhoven, the Netherlands, to pursue my professional doctorate in Human-Machine Interaction and User Experience at the User-System Program at TU/e.

Since then, I gasped different projects that range from online ticketing start-up dashboard, tracking real-time activity data of patients with heart disease or heart failure, engaging seniors to reestablish food-behavior habits with interactive tables and communication of operators in a chemical factory using smart-devices.

In every project, I dive into the user's world.
Passion, comprehension, and empathy are my core guidelines.
User-Centered Design is my motto!

Gather, process and analyze: Data's meaning for business

It doesn't matter if your project is for a university's department, a lab, a factory or a hospital: data is everywhere! In the past decades, technology experienced the evolution of solid-state physics. With transistors, sensors, wireless internet, and smartphones we can track, see and store more data then never. 2016's global internet traffic hit the mark of one zettabyte (or 1 000 000 000 000 000 000 000 bytes)!

I had the opportunity to process and analyze all sorts of data in different projects with the help of DSP methods and data visualization. Using statistics, I could answer hypothesis at distinct scenarios: lab's experiments, usability testing for desktop app's and even while I was investigating the amount of waste produced by a hospital!

My vision

As a UX designer, I'm inspired to explore new ideas that can empower people's life. With a user-centered approach, my purpose is to meet users needs with business goals, to design a successful product.
