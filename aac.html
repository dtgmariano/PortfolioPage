Alternative Communication for people with Severe Motor Dysfunction

ALS Patients, Augmentative and Alternative Communication, Wearables

Summary

Date: January 2014 to July 2015
Clients: University Hospital of Uberlândia (Brazil), University of Uberlândia (Brazil), Université de Lorraine (France)
Service: User research, software design, digital prototyping, data analysis
Team size: 4 members working directly
Duration: 1 year and 7 months
Tools used: .NET, WPF, C#, Excel, SPSS

My roles: (1) Prototype modules for the software; (2) Prototype IHM control using mechanical and physiological sensors, and microcontrollers; (3) Plan and setup experiments for user tests; (4) Data analysis based on user activity log.

Results: Develop a prototype that allowed patients with ALS, hospitalized at the university hospital, to communicate with family members, caregivers and doctors.
